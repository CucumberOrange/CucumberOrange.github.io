Mary Lake Sanctuary


The Mary Lake Sanctuary consists of 42 acres of protected riparian land. It is located in the Highlands District, on the traditional territories of the WS√ÅNEC Coast Salish Peoples, who considered the area to be a place of relaxation. The area has been conserved from the year 1886, and several of its owners have used the area as inspiration for paintings. Today, the Greater Victoria Greenbelt Society and the Tsartlip First Nation have partenered together to restore the land's cultural values. The area lies within the Coastal Douglas Fir ecozone, the smallest and most at-risk ecozone in British Columbia. It is also home to seven unique ecosystems: wetland, aquatic habitats, stream sides, open Douglas Fir forest, canopy Douglas Fir, Douglas Fir/Arbutus Tops and Rock outcrop habitats. On the Second Sunday of May, I went on a self-guided tour of the Sanctuary with my parent on the Lakeview and N. Earsman trails. These trails took us around the edge of the lake to the naturehouse in about 2.5 hours. As proff of this event, I took pictures of the plants, animals and fungi I encountered on the trail, determined their taxonomy and showcased them on this webpage.
